Everything Nurses Ask Before Working With Me

Honest answers to the questions every nurse asks before deciding whether reverse recruiting is right for them.

What exactly does a nurse reverse recruiter do?

I work FOR you instead of for hospitals or agencies. Traditional nursing recruiters are paid by employers to fill their open positions. My loyalty is to you. I study your clinical background, identify the highest paying opportunities that match your specialty, reach out to the right people on your behalf, and coach you through the entire process from resume to signed offer. Think of me as your personal career agent — the same way professional athletes have agents negotiating on their behalf.

How is this different from signing up with a travel nursing agency?

Travel nursing agencies represent the hospitals — not you. They earn their profit from the spread between what a hospital pays them and what they pay you. Their incentive is to fill shifts, not to maximize your pay. I represent only you. I help you identify which agencies are paying the best rates for your specialty, how to negotiate your pay package to its maximum value, and which contracts are worth taking versus which ones to decline.

I have never traveled before. Can you still help me?

Absolutely. Most of my clients are making their first move into travel nursing, remote roles, or advanced practice positions. You do not need prior experience with these pathways — that is exactly what I provide. If you have at least one to two years of clinical experience in an acute care specialty and a current RN license you are likely already qualified for travel nursing contracts. We start with where you are and build from there.

How long does it typically take to land an offer?

Most of my nursing clients receive their first qualified offer within 30 to 60 days of starting the process. Travel nursing contracts can move faster — sometimes within two to three weeks once your profile is positioned correctly. NP and remote case management roles typically take 30 to 60 days. Medical device sales pivots can take 45 to 90 days depending on your specialty and target companies.

My nursing license is in one state. Can I still travel or work remotely?

Many nurses do not realize that 40 plus states participate in the Nurse Licensure Compact — meaning one license gives you the ability to work in multiple states. If your state is a Compact state you are already cleared to travel much more broadly than you think. Remote roles often accept nurses from any state. I help you understand exactly what your current license allows and what steps to take if additional licensure would open better opportunities.

What if I am currently employed? Will this be confidential?

Yes. Many of my clients are actively employed staff nurses who are exploring options confidentially. I never contact your current employer and all outreach on your behalf is handled with complete discretion. Being currently employed actually strengthens your position in most negotiations because you are not searching out of desperation.

I have a two year employment gap. Can you still help me?

Yes. Employment gaps are common in nursing — for family, health, burnout, relocation, and many other reasons. The key is positioning the gap correctly and making sure every other element of your profile is as strong as possible. I have helped nurses with gaps of one to five years successfully land travel contracts and remote roles. What matters more than the gap is your clinical background, your license status, and your specialty experience.
